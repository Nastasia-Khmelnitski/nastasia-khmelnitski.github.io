Coaching Tool

Discovery
Coffee Chat

Before
We Begin
Introduce yourself: your background, what you work on, who you work with, and what brought you to coaching.
Ask whether the client has ever worked with a coach before.
If needed, explain the difference between coaching and mentorship.
01
Opening &
Context
Q
Tell me about where you are right now, professionally and personally, in two or three sentences.
Q
What brought you here specifically, why now and why coaching?
02
The Topic &
Solutions
Q
What is the topic you most want to work on? Formulate a question you have.
Q
What do you think is underneath that question?
Q
What have you already tried?
03
Readiness &
Expectations
Q
What would need to change for this to feel like a success?
Q
What do you expect coaching to do that you cannot do alone?
Q
How motivated are you to work on this topic, on a scale of 1 to 10?
04
Boundaries &
Resistance
Q
What would you not want to change, even if it came up?
Q
Is there anything that is off limits for you in this work?
Q
What would make you decide this is not for you?
05
Closing
the Discovery
Q
Coaching can surface things you did not expect and ask you to sit with discomfort. How do you feel about that?
!
Name a change that coaching can bring that the client does not expect. Be honest about the process before contracting.
06
Contract
Clarify the number of sessions, frequency, and format
Confirm confidentiality and how notes are handled
Agree on what success looks like and how you will measure it
Name what the client commits to between sessions
Confirm that either party can pause or stop the process
